Combat


Declare. Write. Roll.
Declare your attack, write down your new stamina value and ability experience, roll your dice.
Highest stamina attacks first.
Attacks cost 2 stamina and defense moves cost 1 stamina. Attackers can attack until their stamina is used up or they elect to pass(reserve energy).
A combatant's stamina regenerates to full at the beginning of their attack phase.
There is an area in the top right of the character sheet to track your stamina during a battle.

The Attacker uses an offensive ability to attack the Defender. Each ability, offensive or defensive, gains 1 bubble of experience for each use. Upon reaching the 10th bubble, that ability gains a level. The Attacker also declares the Defender's body part that's targetted. There are vital, core, action, and movement body parts. To identify a particular body part, check the mob sheet. Mob sheets will identify targettable body parts for monsters and unusual mobs.
Head (vital body part) -2 attack roll
Chest (core body part) -1 attack roll
Arms (action body part) no penalty
Legs (movement body part) no penalty

The Defender then uses a defensive skill to avoid damage.
Attack roll is 2d6 + offensive skill modifier + dexterity statistic modifier
VS
Defense roll of 2d6 + defensive skill modifier + agility statistic modifier

The Attacker's roll must Beat the Defender's roll to be successful. If the Defender wins, no damage is taken and the attack phase continues. Mobs will stop attacking once their stamina reaches 3 or lower unless otherwise stated on their mob sheet.

If the attack is successful, damage is then calculated against the Defender's armor.
Strength + weapon damage
VS
Agility + armor rating

If the Defender matches or exceeds the Attackers roll, damage is mitigated by the armor and only 1 wound is recieved. If the damage exceeds the armor roll, then the Defender's body part takes 2 wounds from a single handed weapon and 3 wounds from a two handed weapon. Wound bubbles beside each body part are located on the Character sheet.

If the Defender ever runs out of stamina, they automatically take the hit. Once the Attacker runs out of stamina or they elect to pass turn, it is now the Defender's turn to be the Attacker and use their stamina to initiate attacks.

The combatants take turns attacking until any of the following conditions are met:
1 vital body part is incapacitated(fully wounded)
1 core body part is incapacitated
2 action body parts are incapacitated
2 movement body parts are incapacitated

Stumble Mechanic

This mechanic is intended to be used, however, if you would like a more streamlined combat experience you can leave this mechanic out.
Each attack, an extra dice is rolled depending on the Attacker's offensive ability level VS the Defender's defensive ability level. If a 1 is rolled on the stumble dice, you stumble attempting your attack, and the Defender is offered 1 offensive attack. The Attacker is allowed to defend the attack. Stamina is used for both combatants if they chose to attack or defend during this stumble encounter.
Defender ability level > Attacker ability level = 1d8
Attacker A.L. > Defender A.L. = 1d12
Attacker A.L. = Defender A.L. = 1d10

Bow Combat

Bow combat is conducted the same as melee combat, however, there are a few things that are different outlined here.
When comparing damage vs armor rating,
bow's use DEX + Bow Damage.
Bow attacks cost 1 stamina and always do 1 wound if successful. If a bow's attack damage beats the compared armor rating, Bleed is applied to that body part. Bleed is an ailment that will apply 1 wound at the beginning of that mob's attack phase to the body part it is effecting. Bleed can be stopped by a healing spell or potion.
Bows are 2 handed weapons that require a quiver in your off hand to shoot. When swapping to a back weapon, both the bow and quiver will swap to your back.
There is a penalty of -2 to all attack rolls while wearing Platemail and firing a bow.

A few notes on combat:
· If you have 1 of your movement body parts incapacitated, dodge is no longer avaliable for a defensive move.
· If you have 1 of your action body parts incapaciated, 2 handed weapons are no longer avliable for offensive abilities.
· A fully incapacitated action or movement body part can have a potion used on it.
· Weapons, shields, magic tomes, and bows are allowed to be equiped on your back including a quiver.
· When swapping to a back weapon, both main and off hand items may be stored on your back slot. For instance, when equiping a 2 handed maul from your back slot, a 1 handed sword and wooden shield may both be stored on your back.
· All rules apply to players and mobs.
